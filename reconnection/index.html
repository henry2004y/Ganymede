GEM Challenge

This 2D setup is based on Birn+ 2001, maybe with slight modifications.

The computation is carried out in a rectangular domain −Lx/2≤x≤Lx/2-L_x/2 \le x\le L_x/2−Lx​/2≤x≤Lx​/2 and −Lz/2≤z≤Lz/2-L_z/2\le z \le L_z/2−Lz​/2≤z≤Lz​/2. The system is taken to be periodic in the x direction with ideal conducting boundaries at z=±Lz/2z=\pm L_z/2z=±Lz​/2. Thus the boundary conditions on the magnetic fields at the z boundaries are Bz=∂Bx/∂z=∂By/∂z=0B_z=\partial B_x/\partial z = \partial B_y/\partial z = 0Bz​=∂Bx​/∂z=∂By​/∂z=0 with corresponding conditions on th eelectric fields and particle or fluid quantities. (In Vlasiator I use open boundary condition for all quantities.)

The equilibrium chosen for the reconnection challenge problem is a Harris equilibrium with a floor in the density outside of the current layer. The magnetic field is given by

The electron and ion temperatures, TeT_eTe​ and TiT_iTi​, are taken to be uniform in the initial state. We assume the plasma β=(pi+pe)/pB=1\beta = (p_i+p_e)/p_B=1β=(pi​+pe​)/pB​=1 initially.

The initial magnetic island is specified through the perturbation in the magnetic flux,

The rationale for such a large initial perturbation is to put the system in the nonlinear regime of magnetic reconnection from the beginning of the simulation.
